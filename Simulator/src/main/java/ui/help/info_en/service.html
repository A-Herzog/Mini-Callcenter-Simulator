Service

The number of agents indicates how many agents work at the workstation simultaneously.

In the classic call center area, one agent talks to one client at a time. In other areas, however, it may be the case that several clients are served per service batch.

The individual service times of the clients are determined via the service time distribution. While the exponential distribution was often appropriate for inter-arrival times, service times are rarely exponentially distributed. More common are the lognormal or the gamma distribution.

After a conversation, it may be necessary to direct the client to another agent in order to finalize his request. The percentage of served callers to be forwarded after a call can be defined via the input field Forwarding probability.
