An inter-arrival time is the time span between the arrival of two immediately consecutive clients at the system. Average value, standard deviation and coefficients of variation are displayed.

The average inter-arrival time is designated by E[I] (and is measured in seconds). The reciprocal of E[I] is the arrival rate. It is designated by lambda (λ) (measured in 1/second). Small values of E[I] or large values of lambda mean that many clients arrive within a short time.
